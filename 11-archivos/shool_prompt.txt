hool (English: The Trident) is a 1999 Indian Hindi-language action crime film directed by Eeshwar Nivas. 
Written and produced by Ram Gopal Varma, it portrays the politician-criminal nexus and the criminalization of politics in Bihar 
and its effect on the life of an honest police officer. It stars Manoj Bajpayee as Inspector Samar Pratap Singh and Sayaji Shinde 
as the borderline psychopath criminal-politician Bachhu Yadav, a character loosely based on politician turned criminal, Mohammad 
Shahabuddin.[1] The soundtrack consisting of "Main Aayi Hoon U.P. Bihar Lootne" featuring Shilpa Shetty became a 
chartbuster."[2] The rights to this film are owned by Shah Rukh Khan's Red Chillies Entertainment.

Parts of the film were shot in Motihari, Bihar. The climax of the film was entirely shot at the state 
Legislative Assembly in Hyderabad. The film won the National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Hindi,[3] 
and was screened at the International Film Festival of India, and the Toronto International Film Festival.[4][5] 
"India Today" cited the film as the "Best Cop Movie" of the decade.[6]
